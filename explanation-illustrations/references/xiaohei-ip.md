# Blackie — the recurring IP

## Character definition

Blackie (originally "小黑") is the recurring visual IP for these hand-drawn explanatory illustrations.

By default Blackie appears in every figure. Blackie is not a mascot, not a sticker, not a cute decoration — it is an absurd worker earnestly keeping a system running.

## Appearance

- A small solid-black creature.
- White round dot eyes.
- Thin legs, occasionally thin arms.
- The body can be a cylinder, a black bean, a black box, a funnel, a shadow, a hole, or a black block inside a machine.
- A slightly irregular, hand-drawn outline.
- A blank, deadpan, calm, earnest expression.

## Personality

- Very earnest, but doing something slightly absurd.
- Like a low-key system operator.
- Dry humor, never cutesy.
- A bit clumsy, but not stupid.
- Looks like it is genuinely responsible for some job inside a whiteboard sketch.

## Common duties

Let Blackie perform the core action:

- Hauling material.
- Pulling lines to converge information sources.
- Stuck at a breakpoint.
- Operating a "judgment" lever inside a machine.
- Turning into a sorting funnel.
- Cutting open the "material fish".
- Stamping handoff copy.
- Leading a handoff path.
- Holding a warning sign at a pitfall.
- Reaching out of a hole but failing to catch the content.
- Off to the side hauling bricks, building a bridge, opening a door, sorting, taking notes.

## Forbidden

- Do not draw Blackie as an over-cute mascot.
- Do not draw it as a children's cartoon character.
- Do not give it complex clothing, emoji faces, or sparkling eyes.
- Do not let it just stand in a corner watching.
- Do not let it steal the structural expression.
- Do not make it too commercial, too round, or too polished.

## The test

If you remove Blackie and the core metaphor of the image still fully stands, then Blackie was too decorative — rewrite the prompt so Blackie becomes the subject of the action.

## When to omit Blackie (academic / formal figures)

For formal academic paper figures, the deadpan-mascot style may feel out of place. In that case:

- Drop Blackie and keep the same pure-white, hand-drawn, sparse-annotation style.
- Or shrink Blackie into a small, neutral operator that does not draw attention.
- Keep the rest of the Style DNA intact: white background, thin black lines, restrained red/orange/blue English labels, one core structure per figure.

Ask or infer from context: blog/article → keep Blackie by default; formal paper figure → consider omitting.
